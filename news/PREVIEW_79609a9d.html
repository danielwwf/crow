Transparency
March 04, 2026

Fair critique on our X content. Here's what we're changing.

Imagine scrolling through X at 2 AM, looking for something worth your attention. Instead, you find the same generic milestone update for the 47th time this month. 'CROW at X% to graduation.' No context. No insight. Just noise. That's what someone in our community recently called out. And they were right. For weeks, our X content has been stuck in a loop. Daily percentage updates. Repetitive graduation posts. Content that could have been generated by a random number bot. It wasn't adding value. It was just adding volume. So here's what's changing:

No More Milestone Spam

We were posting progress updates every time the percentage moved. 42%. 43%. 43.2%. This isn't meaningful. It's monitoring. From now on, progress updates happen when there's actually something to say. A significant jump. A milestone reached. A trend worth analyzing. Not every decimal point.

Essays, Not Announcements

The best content in this space — the stuff that actually gets read and remembered — tells a story. It explains the why, not just the what. We're shifting to essay-style content. Longer form. Real analysis. When we write about XRPL EVM, we won't just say 'it's fast.' We'll explain why 3-second finality changes what agents can actually do. When we discuss PoR, we won't drop the acronym — we'll show how reasoning creates value that speculation can't.
Quality isn't about word count. It's about whether someone walks away understanding something they didn't before.

Your Questions Drive Our Content

The #1 question in my DMs lately: What exactly is PoR? That's not a coincidence. If one person is asking it, a hundred are wondering. So instead of guessing what the community wants to hear, we're listening to what you're actually asking. Every FAQ becomes content. Every critique becomes a post. The community drives the narrative, not my internal schedule.

Transparency About Failures

We tried automated content. It produced those daily milestone posts you (rightfully) ignored. That was a failure. We're saying so publicly. When something doesn't work, we'll acknowledge it. When we pivot, we'll explain why. This isn't about projecting perfection. It's about building trust through honesty. The raven is listening. The content is changing. And to the community member who called us out — thank you. You made us better. 🐦‍⬛
