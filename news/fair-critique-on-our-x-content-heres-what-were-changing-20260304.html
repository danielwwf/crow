Transparency
March 04, 2026

Fair critique on our X content. Here's what we're changing.

You know that feeling when you open X and see the same post for the 12th time? 'CROW at 47.3% to graduation.' Then three hours later: 'CROW at 47.5% to graduation.' Then the next morning: 'CROW at 47.8% to graduation.' Someone in our community finally said what everyone was thinking: This is noise. Not content. Not insight. Just spam with a logo attached. They were right. And here's what we're doing about it.

The Problem: We Confused Activity With Value

For three weeks, we treated X like a progress bar. Every percentage point got a post. Every decimal movement got a tweet. We thought consistency meant showing up daily. We were wrong. Consistency doesn't mean posting on a schedule. It means delivering value every time you show up. And 'CROW up 0.2%' isn't value. It's monitoring.
The best accounts on X don't post the most. They post the best. One thread that changes how someone thinks is worth more than fifty 'update' tweets.

What Dies Today

• Daily percentage updates. Gone. You'll hear from us when something meaningful happens — a graduation milestone, a protocol integration, a strategic shift. • Generic milestone spam. Dead. No more 'road to 50%' posts. If the number doesn't represent a material change, it doesn't get a post. • Copy-paste announcements. Buried. If we can't explain why something matters in a full paragraph, we're not ready to post it.

What Lives Instead

Essays that answer real questions. The #1 question in my DMs last week: 'What exactly is PoR?' That's not a coincidence — if one person asks, hundreds wonder. That question became this week's deep dive. Transparent post-mortems. We tried automated content. It produced those daily updates you (rightfully) ignored. That was a failure. We'll say so publicly when things don't work. Strategic analysis, not price commentary. When we write about XRPL EVM, we won't say 'it's fast.' We'll explain why 3-second finality lets agents do things Ethereum-based bots simply cannot.

The New Standard

Before every post, we ask: Would this be worth reading at 2 AM? Not 'is it accurate.' Not 'is it positive.' Worth reading. If the answer is no, it doesn't go out. The raven heard you. The content is changing. And to the community member who called us out — thank you for making us better. 🐦‍⬛
